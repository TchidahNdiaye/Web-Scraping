<meta name="description" content="Through eight cases collected in the province of Zhejiang, this thesis aims to reconstruct the evolution of financial methods adopted by the private entrepreneurs during the reform era. On the one hand, the private entrepreneurs are adapted continuously to the changing juridical environment which determines the frontier of the formal financial space; on the other hand, they also participate actively in the construction of informal institutions composed by the rules, customs and social Dorms in order to satisfy their financial needs which evolve constantly with the transformation of the organization of their economic activities. During this process, some institutional innovations are formalized by the local government and legally institutionalized by the State, while others are prohibited to exist and can not develop gradually with the private economy that they serve. We thus are confronted with the complexity of institutional transformation in contemporary China and question the efficiency of Chine se political system, which neglect the plurality of individual behavior, the diversity of financial needs and the variety of institutional arrangement" xml:lang="EN"/>

            <h2>Résumé</h2>
            <p><span property="dc:description" xml:lang="fr">Au travers de huit cas recueillis dans la province de Zhejiang qui mettent en lumière l'évolution des modalités de financement des entrepreneurs privés, cette thèse vise à démontrer que les entrepreneurs privés, d'un côté, sont adaptés continuellement à l'environnement juridique changeant qui détermine l' étendue de l'espace financier formel; d' un autre côté, participent à la construction des institutions informelles composées des règles de conduite, des coutumes et des normes sociales pour leur besoin financier qui évolue concomitamment avec la transformation de l'organisation des activités économiques privées. Pendant ce processus, certaines innovations institutionnelles sont formalisées par le gouvernement local et institutionnalisées juridiquement par l'État, alors que d'autres sont interdites d'exister légalement et sont condamnées de ne pouvoir pas se moderniser en réponse du développement de l'économie privée qu’ils servent. Nous constatons donc la complexité du changement institutionnel en Chine contemporaine et interrogeons l'efficacité du système politique chinois qui néglige la pluralité des comportements individuels, la diversité des besoins financiers et la variété des situations d’institutions</span></p></li>

          <h2>Titre traduit </h2>
            <p><span property="dcterms:alternative" xml:lang="en">Financial space and development of private enterprises in contemporary China : case study in Zhejiang</span></p></li>
          <li>
<br />
            <h2>Résumé</h2>
            <p><span property="dc:description" xml:lang="en">Through eight cases collected in the province of Zhejiang, this thesis aims to reconstruct the evolution of financial methods adopted by the private entrepreneurs during the reform era. On the one hand, the private entrepreneurs are adapted continuously to the changing juridical environment which determines the frontier of the formal financial space; on the other hand, they also participate actively in the construction of informal institutions composed by the rules, customs and social Dorms in order to satisfy their financial needs which evolve constantly with the transformation of the organization of their economic activities. During this process, some institutional innovations are formalized by the local government and legally institutionalized by the State, while others are prohibited to exist and can not develop gradually with the private economy that they serve. We thus are confronted with the complexity of institutional transformation in contemporary China and question the efficiency of Chine se political system, which neglect the plurality of individual behavior, the diversity of financial needs and the variety of institutional arrangement</span></p></li>
