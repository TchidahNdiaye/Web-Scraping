The first part is an attempt to capture the prevailing of investor sentiment on French Market index CAC ALLTRADABLE during the period 2000- 2015. The research's main objective is to study investor's time-varying preferences for dividend-paying firms across sectors. That's why, an examination of catering hypothesis's validity will be treated, after adjusting the probability to pay dividends with the key determinant such as maturity, firm size.   In the second part, a great emphasis will be mainly put on examining the cause effect relationship between both concepts dividend policy and CEO overconfidence. In other words, could managerial bias (such as overconfidence) and corporate governance provide a better explanation for dividend policy decisions?  The third part will shed light on combine effect of investor sentiment and managerial biases on dividend policy. we try to match the irrational approaches to give a better understanding of the corporate firm's decisions. Therefore, the mixture of these two biases and their impact on dividend policy has not been tackled yet on financial literature." xml:lang="EN"/>

            <h2>Résumé</h2>
            <p><span property="dc:description" xml:lang="fr">Malgré, les nombreuses recherches théoriques et empiriques se rapportant à la politique de dividendes, elle continu à être l'une des problématiques les plus discutés dans la littérature financière.   Les théories classiques reposent implicitement sur l'hypothèse de l'efficience des marchés financiers, ces théories ne peuvent plus expliquer à elles seules le comportement de la politique de dividende, puisqu'elles n'évoquent pas la dimension psychologique et émotionnelle relatives aux préférences des investisseurs.      Toutefois, la thèse s'efforce d'exploiter l'irrationalité des individus issus de l'approche comportementale de la finance pour expliquer les décisions financières en occurrence la politique de dividendes.</span></p></li>

          <h2>Titre traduit </h2>
            <p><span property="dcterms:alternative" xml:lang="en">Dividend Policy, Irrationality approaches and  Behavioral Corporate Finance: Theory and Evidence</span></p></li>
          <li>
<br />
            <h2>Résumé</h2>
            <p><span property="dc:description" xml:lang="en">This thesis is divided into three major sections. The first part is an attempt to capture the prevailing of investor sentiment on French Market index CAC ALLTRADABLE during the period 2000- 2015. The research's main objective is to study investor's time-varying preferences for dividend-paying firms across sectors. That's why, an examination of catering hypothesis's validity will be treated, after adjusting the probability to pay dividends with the key determinant such as maturity, firm size.   In the second part, a great emphasis will be mainly put on examining the cause effect relationship between both concepts dividend policy and CEO overconfidence. In other words, could managerial bias (such as overconfidence) and corporate governance provide a better explanation for dividend policy decisions?  The third part will shed light on combine effect of investor sentiment and managerial biases on dividend policy. we try to match the irrational approaches to give a better understanding of the corporate firm's decisions. Therefore, the mixture of these two biases and their impact on dividend policy has not been tackled yet on financial literature.</span></p></li>
